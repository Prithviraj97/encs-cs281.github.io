Course Structure

What programming language will we use in this course?

This course primarily uses C++ as the main programming language. We've chosen C++ because it provides a good balance between performance, features, and relevance to industry. However, the principles and algorithms you'll learn can be applied to many other programming languages.

Do I need to have taken CS180 to succeed in this course?

Yes, CS180 (Intro to Computer Science I) or an equivalent course is a prerequisite for CS281. You should be comfortable with basic programming concepts such as variables, control structures, functions, and simple data structures. If you have not taken CS180 but believe you have equivalent knowledge, please contact the instructor to discuss your situation.

What is the attendance policy?

Regular attendance is highly encouraged and correlated with success in the course. While we don't have a strict attendance requirement for lectures, participation in discussions and in-class activities will contribute to your overall grade. Lab sessions have mandatory attendance, and unexcused absences will affect your lab grade.

Assignments & Projects

How are assignments submitted?

All assignments and projects will be submitted through GitHub Classroom. You'll receive a GitHub repository link for each assignment, where you'll push your code. Make sure to follow the submission instructions carefully, as automated grading scripts will check your submissions. You'll find detailed submission instructions in each assignment document.

What is the late submission policy?

Assignments submitted after the deadline will incur a 10% penalty per day for up to three days. After three days, submissions will not be accepted without prior approval. Each student receives three "late days" that can be used throughout the semester without penalty. To use a late day, you must notify the instructor before the assignment deadline.

Can I work with a partner on assignments?

Some assignments will be designated as partner assignments, while others must be completed individually. The assignment descriptions will clearly state whether collaboration is permitted. For individual assignments, you must write your own code. For partner assignments, you and your partner should submit a single solution with both names. Please review the course syllabus for our collaboration policy.

Exams & Grading

How many exams are there in this course?

The course has two midterm exams and one final exam. The midterms will cover material up to that point in the semester, while the final exam is comprehensive. Each midterm is worth 15% of your final grade, and the final exam is worth 25%.

What is the format of the exams?

Exams will include a mix of multiple-choice questions, short answer questions, and coding problems. You'll be tested on theoretical concepts as well as practical application of those concepts. Coding problems will require you to write or analyze code by hand. No electronic devices are permitted during exams.

Is there a curve for this course?

The course uses absolute grading rather than curve-based grading. This means your grade is determined by your performance against set criteria, not by comparison to other students. The grade cutoffs are: A (90-100%), B (80-89%), C (70-79%), D (60-69%), and F (below 60%). Plus and minus grades may be assigned at the instructor's discretion.

Technical Issues

What software do I need for this course?

You'll need a C++ compiler (such as GCC, Clang, or Visual C++), a code editor or IDE (we recommend Visual Studio Code or CLion), and Git for version control. Detailed setup instructions are available on the Resources page. All the required software is free and available for Windows, macOS, and Linux.

Can I use the lab computers for assignments?

Yes, the department computer labs have all the necessary software installed. Lab hours are posted on the course website, and you can use the labs during open hours. However, for your convenience, we recommend setting up your personal computer with the required software as well.

I'm having trouble with my code. Where can I get help?

There are several resources available:

  • Office hours with the instructor and TAs
  • The course discussion forum (monitored by the teaching staff)
  • Peer tutoring in the CS Learning Center
  • Online resources listed on the Resources page

For debugging issues, please make sure to include specific error messages and a minimal example that demonstrates the problem.
